I am a MS student at the University of Chicago in statistics advised by Prof. Xinran Li. Previously, I obtained B.Sc. in Risk Management Science with First Class Honors from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, with a minor in Statistics. My current research interest lies in both theory and application for causal inference and semiparametric models.

Projects

  • Adaptive Covariate Adjustment in Stratified Randomized Experiments across Strata Sizes
    Oct 2023 – present

    We proposed an adaptive covariate adjustment on Cox model in stratified randomized experiments. I proved the asymptotic properties of our adjusted difference-in-means estimator.

  • Federated Learning Adjusting for Hidden Confounding
    Mar 2024 – present

    We designed a semiparametric approach for federated learning and adjusted for the hidden confounding.

  • A Semiparametric Approach for Robust Modeling of Electronic Health Record Linked Biobank Data
    May 2022 – April 2023

    We designed a semiparametric approach for robust modeling of EHR Data. I proved the asymptotic properties of the proposed estimators and ran simulation studies for three settings.

  • An Adaptive Test on Cox Model
    Jul 2022 – Oct 2022

    We proposed an adaptive test on Cox model. I proved the convergence to zero of the third moment of Cox score function under i.i.d. condition.

  • Transformed Dynamic Quantile Regression under Biased Sampling
    Jul 2021 – Oct 2022

    • We developed a transformed dynamic estimation method of quantile regression under biased sampling. I proved asymptotic properties of the proposed estimators and the wild bootstrap method using product integral.
