Projects

  • Design and analysis of iterative algorithms for nonconvex optimization problems
    Jun 2021 – present, at Institute of Theoretical Computer Science and Communications

    We proved a linear convergence guarantee for a first-order iterative method, i.e., the generalized power method (GPM), to solve the joint optimization problem of community detection and group synchronization. The general strategy was to show the contraction-mapping property of each iteration by establishing the Lipschitz property on the projection operator onto the nonconvex feasible set. As a result of our theoretical development, the algorithm is able to recover the ground truth within \(O(n\log^2n)\) time, with a huge lift from the previous method of semidefinite relaxation with \(O(n^{3.5})\) time complexity. I played a major role in proving the guarantee and explored a lot of mathematical tools such as matrix theory, random graphs, numerical analysis, and group theory. Meanwhile, I had a great fun attending the lunch gatherings with professors in ITCSC!
